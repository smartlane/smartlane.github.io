Styling your geospatial data directly and though augmentation

By Matthew Fullerton
2015-09-28

If you export your data as geojson with Mapbox styling information, SMARTLANE will use the attributes to style the map objects. In the spirit of separating content from style, you can provide your geojson without style information and have our augmentation service fill the attributes with a value based on another JSON mapping.



How does it work?

To see this in action, let's start with a geojson resource on smartlane.io containing traffic light positions. We are already using styles here to affect point sizes and borders, but the colors are all the same.

To see the results, check out either our traffic lights demo at https://www.smartlane.io/dataset/traffic-light-stream-frankfurt-niederrad developed together with the City of Frankfurt, or a (more occasionally updated) map of the traffic flow quality in New York: https://smartlane.io/dataset/nyc-dept-of-transport-real-time-traffic-speed-data/resource/c2511f93-60eb-45e1-817a-93612ca32b26.
